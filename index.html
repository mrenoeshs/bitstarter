Technology is changing society in amazing ways. We have the world at our fingertips since we can access the internet anywhere and at anytime with our mobile devices. There is a plethora of apps that make our lives better and technology is changing the way that we do things on a daily basis. Despite all of these amazing changes students are still learning through archaic methods. Most students are stuck reading through textbooks and are forced to learn on someone else's schedule. Student's don't feel a connection to the material they are learning and get stuck in a cycle of failure. Our goal at Grasp is to use technology to help students become invested in their education and enter into a cycle of succes. We want to give them a chance to become emotionally invested in their learning by allowing them to track their progress in a gamified environment that they interact with on an hourly basis.

The research is clear that students need to be engaged behaviorally, emotionally, and cognitively. While some schools can handle one or two components of engagement, there are very few who can handle all three. With the Common Core Standards on their way we need to find ways to build student confidence. Through the work of the Center for Math and Science Teaching (CMAST) at Loyola Marymount University a group of highly trained and dedicated teachers have created a methodology to keep students engaged on all three levels. The progress has been amazing and students involved in the program are learning more than they thought possible. The teachers from CMAST can see there is a lot of room for improvement, especially around tracking student progress and engagement. Grasp solves these issues by putting the responsibility for tracking learning in the students hands. They now can earn points and medals by setting goals, completing To-Do lists, and enjoying learning.

FAQ

What is Grasp?
Grasp is a quantified learning application to help students and teachers track learning in a fun and engaging way.
What is CMAST?
The Center for Math and Science Teaching is dedicated to strengthening science, technology, engineering, mathematics (STEM) and STEM-related fields by transforming the teaching and learning of mathematics and science.
